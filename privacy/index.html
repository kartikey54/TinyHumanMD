1. Purpose and Framing

This Privacy Policy explains how TinyHumanMD approaches information handling, legal boundaries, and user responsibility. TinyHumanMD is a clinical decision-support reference site, not a healthcare delivery service, not an electronic health record platform, and not a regulated treatment system. The policy is written to reduce ambiguity about ownership of risk, compliance obligations, and implementation accountability.

When this policy refers to "we" or "TinyHumanMD," it refers to the site operator. When this policy refers to "you," it includes both individual users and institutions on whose behalf those users act. If an individual is using the site in the course of employment, fellowship, residency, or contracted clinical work, institutional obligations remain fully in effect.

2. Scope

This policy applies to content, calculators, pages, and materials served under the TinyHumanMD web property. It does not govern external websites, third-party references, or software repositories not controlled by the site operator, even if linked from this site.

The policy addresses privacy posture, data-boundary design, and compliance responsibility. It does not provide legal advice. Users needing jurisdiction-specific counsel should consult licensed attorneys with healthcare and data-governance expertise.

3. Definitions

Personal Information means information that identifies, relates to, describes, can reasonably be associated with, or could reasonably be linked with a particular person or household under applicable law. Sensitive Data means categories treated as sensitive by law, regulation, or institutional policy. PHI means Protected Health Information as defined in HIPAA where applicable.

Local Environment means your own browser, endpoint, network, and institution-managed systems. Third-Party Infrastructure means service providers not directly operated by TinyHumanMD. Operational Use means using outputs in clinical, compliance, billing, policy, or workflow decisions.

4. Service Characterization

TinyHumanMD is positioned as an informational decision-support interface. It is not a physician practice, not a hospital service line, not a pharmacy verification authority, and not a substitute for licensed clinical judgment. Calculator outputs are computational aids that require independent interpretation and validation.

Nothing in this policy should be construed as a promise that site use, by itself, satisfies statutory compliance, accreditation readiness, or payer obligations. Institutions must run their own governance controls, risk reviews, and supervisory approvals.

5. No Account-Centric Custody Model

TinyHumanMD is designed for public access without mandatory account creation for core tool use. Because the site is not an account-based charting platform, users must not assume institutional-grade custodial obligations are being performed by default.

If your workflow requires managed identities, enterprise audit logs, immutable retention, legal hold procedures, or regulated archival guarantees, you must use tools and contracts that explicitly provide those functions.

6. User Input Responsibility

You are solely responsible for everything you enter, paste, import, export, and operationalize. This includes demographic values, unit conventions, timestamps, gestational assumptions, medication concentrations, and any contextual notes used to interpret outputs.

You must not enter information you are not authorized to process. If your institution prohibits entry of specific data classes into non-approved systems, those restrictions control. A user-side violation remains a user-side compliance failure.

7. Data Minimization Expectations

Use the least amount of information necessary to achieve your immediate lawful purpose. Do not include direct identifiers unless required and explicitly permitted by policy. Do not treat convenience as a justification for over-collection.

Data minimization is both a design principle and a user behavior requirement. Policy-compliant outcomes depend on your operational discipline, not merely on website text.

8. Endpoint and Browser Controls

Endpoint security is your responsibility. You must apply reasonable controls over device locking, credential handling, local session hygiene, browser profile separation, and network transport integrity. Shared workstation use requires heightened procedural safeguards.

TinyHumanMD is not responsible for shoulder-surfing, unattended sessions, endpoint malware, insecure local storage practices, or unauthorized access resulting from user environment weaknesses.

9. Institution-Owned Governance Duties

Institutions remain responsible for policy mapping, competency validation, supervision rules, escalation thresholds, incident handling, audit readiness, and quality-improvement loops. TinyHumanMD does not substitute for a formal governance framework.

If your environment requires committee review, policy sign-off, legal attestation, or procurement approval before external-tool use, those requirements remain mandatory and supersede convenience.

10. HIPAA and BAA Position

TinyHumanMD is not represented as a HIPAA covered-entity service, not represented as a Business Associate service, and not represented as a turnkey HIPAA-compliant record platform. Website access does not create a Business Associate Agreement.

If your use case requires contractual HIPAA assurances, you must secure them through separate legal arrangements with approved vendors and counsel. Do not infer contractual coverage from public availability.

11. Third-Party Dependencies

Site operation may rely on third-party delivery or infrastructure services. Those providers maintain independent terms and privacy policies. TinyHumanMD does not guarantee that third-party controls satisfy your organization’s procurement, compliance, or residency requirements.

Where external references are provided, they are informational. Linking does not create endorsement, warranty, agency, or legal adoption of third-party obligations.

12. Cross-Border Use

Users accessing the site from outside their primary jurisdiction are responsible for compliance with local laws governing data transfer, professional licensing, and health-information handling. Not every jurisdiction treats healthcare-adjacent tools identically.

Cross-border legal compatibility should be assessed by your legal and compliance teams before operational use. This policy is not a substitute for transfer impact assessment or jurisdiction-specific counsel.

13. US State Privacy Overlay

Where U.S. state privacy laws apply, users may have rights including access, correction, deletion, portability, and appeal, subject to scope limitations, exemptions, and verification requirements. Rights availability varies by state and by context of processing.

Requests can be submitted through the official contact channel. Responses will follow applicable law for data actually held by the site operator and lawfully discoverable through reasonable verification steps.

14. UK/EU Rights Overlay

For users in the UK or EEA, applicable data-protection frameworks may provide rights such as access, rectification, erasure, restriction, objection, and complaint rights with supervisory authorities. These rights are subject to statutory qualifications and lawful exceptions.

Where a right request is made, the site operator may require sufficient identity verification and may reject, defer, or narrow requests where legally permitted, disproportionate, abusive, or unsupported by available records.

15. Children and Vulnerable Users

TinyHumanMD is intended for trained adults and is not directed to children. If a minor or unauthorized user accesses the site, responsible supervisors and guardians remain accountable for lawful use decisions.

No section of this policy authorizes minors to provide regulated information through unsupervised use.

16. Security Disclosures and Residual Risk

No internet-delivered system can guarantee absolute confidentiality, integrity, or availability. Users accept residual risk including provider incidents, software defects, endpoint compromise, and human factors.

Where incidents occur, institutions remain responsible for their own triage, containment, notification, and remediation duties required by law or policy.

17. Retention and Deletion Boundaries

TinyHumanMD does not replace institutional retention policy. Any records your institution creates by copying or operationalizing outputs remain institution-owned records subject to institution-owned retention schedules and legal-hold duties.

If local browser artifacts exist in user environments, users and institutions are responsible for secure deletion and lifecycle control consistent with internal policy.

18. Audit and Documentation Responsibilities

Where operational decisions are influenced by site outputs, institutions should maintain their own audit trail showing who reviewed data, what references were checked, what discrepancies were resolved, and what supervisory approvals were obtained.

TinyHumanMD does not guarantee that public site access logs, if any, satisfy your evidentiary or compliance burden. You must implement your own audit architecture.

19. Policy Changes

This policy may be modified at any time. Updated language is effective upon posting with revised dates. Continued use after posting indicates acceptance of updated terms.

If your organization requires change management with fixed legal baselines, maintain internal snapshots and approval processes rather than relying on mutable public pages as sole source of truth.

20. Contact and Requests

Privacy questions and rights requests may be submitted via the official repository or site contact channel. Response timelines and response scope are governed by applicable law, request clarity, and verification feasibility.

To protect users, requests may require additional proof of authority where the request concerns institutional or regulated contexts.

21. Interpretation and Priority

This policy should be interpreted consistently with the Terms of Use and Medical Disclaimer. Where provisions appear to conflict, interpretation should preserve the site’s non-treatment status and user-side implementation responsibility, except where mandatory law requires otherwise.

Nothing here creates third-party beneficiary rights or implied service guarantees.

22. Final Responsibility Statement

TinyHumanMD provides informational support. Final responsibility for legal compliance, clinical verification, policy governance, and implementation outcomes remains with users and their institutions.

If your use case requires contractual assurance of enterprise controls, audited compliance, or legally guaranteed data-processing architecture, you must procure systems designed and contracted for that purpose.
